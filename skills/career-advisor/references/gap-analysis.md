## Dream Job Definition

**Don't start with a job title. Start with conditions.**

Guide the user through these questions:

1. **Daily reality**: What does a great day in this role look like, hour by hour?
2. **People**: Who do you work with — peers, reports, leadership? What's the caliber of the room?
3. **Problems**: What problems are you solving? At what scale?
4. **Success**: What does "winning" look like in year one? Year three?
5. **Title**: What's the title that reflects that reality — and what companies hire for it?

From the answers, help identify 3-5 real job postings that represent the dream. Save them in `opportunities/` tagged `[DREAM]`.

## Running the Gap Analysis

With the user's accomplishments and dream JDs in hand, analyze across four dimensions:

| Dimension | What to Look For |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Technical skills** | Tools, platforms, languages, frameworks required but absent |
| **Domain knowledge** | Industry depth, functional expertise the user hasn't operated in |
| **Scope & scale** | Team sizes, budget sizes, organizational complexity not yet held |
| **Credentials & signals** | Degrees, certifications, titles, publications typically expected |

### Gap Classification

For each gap, classify:

| Type | Meaning | Closing Effort |
|------|---------|----------------|
| `HARD` | Gate the hiring manager will not pass you through without it | Need a closing plan |
| `SOFT` | Preference that can be offset by demonstrated strength elsewhere | Need a story |
| `NARRATIVE` | You have the substance but haven't framed it in their language | Close in an afternoon |

## Closing Tracks

Courses are the slowest, lowest-signal way to close a gap. Map the full landscape first:

### Track 1: Formal Learning
Courses and certifications when the **credential itself** is the signal. Best for `HARD` gaps where the paper matters more than the knowledge.

**When to use**: Required certifications (AWS SA, PMP, CPA), degree requirements, regulated industries.
**Signal strength**: High for gated roles, low for skill-based roles.

### Track 2: Build Something
Side projects, open source contributions, proof-of-concept systems. Best for technical gaps where **demonstrated ability outweighs claimed knowledge**.

**When to use**: Missing a technology skill, need portfolio evidence, want to learn by doing.
**Signal strength**: Very high for technical roles. A deployed project > a certificate.
**Key**: Solve a real problem you actually have. Authenticity shows.

### Track 3: Earn Reps
Freelance, consulting, contract roles, advisory positions. Best for **scope and scale gaps**.

**When to use**: Need to claim leadership of a larger team, need industry experience, need revenue responsibility.
**Signal strength**: Highest. Real work produces real STAR stories.
**Key**: Even a short engagement produces a legitimate story.

### Track 4: Write or Teach
Articles, talks, workshops, courses on the gap topic. Closes **visibility gaps** and signals depth simultaneously.

**When to use**: Need to be known for something, want to deepen understanding, building personal brand.
**Signal strength**: High for thought leadership roles. Forces genuine learning.

### Track 5: Network Proximity
Get adjacent to people already doing the dream job. Informational interviews, communities, conferences.

**When to use**: Need referrals, need real intelligence about what the role requires, need to understand culture.
**Signal strength**: Indirect but powerful. Proximity produces referrals.

### Track 6: Reframe What You Have
Before closing a gap, check if it's really a narrative gap in disguise. Many people have the experience and haven't named it in the target language.

**When to use**: Always check this first. If you've done FinOps work inside a broader cloud role, the gap isn't experience — it's vocabulary.
**Signal strength**: Instant. Just rewrite the story.

## Prioritization & Timeline

Not all gaps need to close before applying. Sequence them:

| Tier | Gap Types | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Apply now** | `SOFT` or `NARRATIVE` | Apply, address in cover/interview, let strengths carry |
| **30-60 days** | Quick closes: a short course, a reframe, a side project | Keep applications warm |
| **90-180 days** | Gaps needing reps or a credential with a timeline | Warm the target companies |
| **6-12 months** | Hard structural gaps (degree, specific industry tenure) | Decide if worth the runway |
| **Never** | Gap costs more than the role is worth | Redefine the dream |
