## Core Philosophy

**"Don't start with job titles. Start with who you are."**

Most career advice begins with "what jobs are available?" This system begins with "what makes you come alive?" The market changes. Job titles change. Who you are is the constant.

Key principles:
- Identity is not a job title. You are not "a developer" or "a manager" — you are a person who builds, leads, teaches, creates.
- Roles are vehicles, not destinations. A startup founder and a corporate VP might both be driven by the same core need: building systems that scale.
- Deliberate choice > default path. The goal is not to find the "right" career — it's to make conscious choices knowing the trade-offs.
- Multiple valid paths exist. Students, parents returning to work, mid-career changers, and executives all benefit from the same clarity process.

## The Ikigai Framework

Ikigai sits at the intersection of four circles. Guide the user through each:

```
         What you LOVE
              │
    ┌─────────┼─────────┐
    │   Passion│  Mission│
    │         ┌┴┐        │
What you ────┤☆├──── What the
are GOOD at  └┬┘    world NEEDS
    │  Profession│Vocation│
    └─────────┼─────────┘
              │
     What you can be
        PAID for
```

### Circle 1: What You Love (Passions)

Questions to explore:
- What activities make you lose track of time?
- What topics do you read about voluntarily?
- What would you do on a free Saturday with no obligations?
- When have you felt most energized at work? What were you doing?
- What problems fascinate you even when they're hard?
- If money were irrelevant, what would you spend your days doing?

Capture format:
```
| Passion | Evidence | Energy Level (1-5) |
|---------|----------|--------------------|
```

### Circle 2: What You're Good At (Skills & Strengths)

Two categories:
- **Hard skills**: Technical capabilities, certifications, tools, languages
- **Soft skills**: Communication, leadership, problem-solving, creativity

Questions:
- What do people consistently ask you for help with?
- What comes easy to you that others find hard?
- What have you been praised for in reviews or feedback?
- What skills have you built over years of practice?
- What can you teach someone else to do?

Capture format:
```
| Skill | Proficiency (1-5) | Evidence | Others Confirm? |
|-------|--------------------|----------|-----------------|
```

### Circle 3: What the World Needs (Market Demand)

Questions:
- What problems do companies pay to solve in your domain?
- What skills appear repeatedly in job postings you find interesting?
- What trends are growing in your industry?
- What gaps do you see that nobody is filling?
- What do customers/users complain about that you could fix?

Capture format:
```
| Need / Problem | Who Pays for It | Growing or Shrinking | Your Connection |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|-----------------|
```

### Circle 4: What You Can Be Paid For (Viable Careers)

Questions:
- What roles combine your skills with market demand?
- What's the salary range for work you'd enjoy?
- What business models exist around your expertise? (employment, freelance, teaching, products)
- Who is already doing what you want to do? What path did they take?

Capture format:
```
| Career Path | Compensation Range | Entry Requirements | Your Readiness |
|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------|
```

### Finding the Intersection

After completing all four circles, identify patterns:

| Zone | Intersection | Feeling |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Passion** | Love + Good at | Satisfaction but no income |
| **Mission** | Love + World needs | Fulfillment but may struggle financially |
| **Vocation** | World needs + Paid for | Useful but may feel empty |
| **Profession** | Good at + Paid for | Comfortable but may feel purposeless |
| **Ikigai** | All four | Purpose, mastery, contribution, sustainability |

Help the user map their entries and identify where they cluster. Perfect ikigai is rare — the goal is to move toward it, not demand perfection.

## Values Discovery

Values guide decisions when everything else is equal. Help users identify their top 5-7.

### Values Assessment Process

Step 1 — Broad selection from this list (pick all that resonate):

```
Achievement    Adventure     Autonomy      Balance
Belonging      Challenge     Collaboration Community
Creativity     Curiosity     Excellence    Fairness
Family         Freedom       Growth        Health
Honesty        Impact        Independence  Innovation
Integrity      Justice       Kindness      Knowledge
Leadership     Learning      Legacy        Loyalty
Mastery        Meaning       Mentorship    Nature
Openness       Passion       Peace         Persistence
Power          Purpose       Recognition   Reliability
Respect        Responsibility Security     Service
Simplicity     Spirituality  Stability     Status
Teaching       Teamwork      Tradition     Trust
Vision         Wealth        Wisdom
```

Step 2 — Narrow to top 10 by forced ranking (if you could only keep 10...)

Step 3 — Final 5-7 with definitions:
```
| Rank | Value | What It Means to Me | Evidence From My Life |
|------|-------|---------------------|-----------------------|
```

Step 4 — Values alignment test:
- Does your current/target role honor your top 5 values?
- Which values are being violated? That's the source of dissatisfaction.
- Which values are non-negotiable vs. flexible?

## Identity Exploration

### Beyond Job Titles

Guide users away from "I am a [title]" toward "I am someone who [verbs]."

Exercise: Complete these sentences:
- "I am someone who..." (list 10 things, no job titles allowed)
- "I feel most like myself when..."
- "People come to me when they need..."
- "I can't stop myself from..."
- "The common thread across everything I've done is..."

### Role Archetypes (Not Boxes)

Present these as lenses, not labels. Most people are a blend:

| Archetype | Core Drive | Looks Like |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Builder** | Creating from nothing | Founder, engineer, artist, architect |
| **Optimizer** | Making things better | Consultant, analyst, process engineer |
| **Connector** | Linking people and ideas | Sales, community, partnerships, teaching |
| **Guardian** | Protecting and sustaining | Security, compliance, operations, QA |
| **Explorer** | Discovering the unknown | Researcher, strategist, journalist, scout |
| **Mentor** | Developing others | Teacher, coach, manager, advisor |
| **Advocate** | Fighting for a cause | Nonprofit, policy, DEI, social enterprise |

Questions:
- Which 2-3 archetypes feel most natural?
- How have these shown up across different roles you've held?
- What combination would your ideal role activate?

### Life Stages and Context

Career identity shifts with life context. Acknowledge this:

| Stage | Typical Priority | Key Question |
|-------|-----------------|--------------|
| Student / early career | Exploration, skill building | "What can I try?" |
| Young professional | Growth, financial foundation | "What am I good at?" |
| Parent / caregiver | Stability, flexibility, meaning | "What works for my life?" |
| Mid-career | Impact, mastery, legacy | "Is this still me?" |
| Career changer | Reinvention, courage, transfer | "What transfers?" |
| Late career | Legacy, mentorship, purpose | "What do I leave behind?" |

## Skills Inventory

### Comprehensive Skills Mapping

Help users build a complete picture:

**Technical / Hard Skills**
```
| Skill | Years | Proficiency (1-5) | Formal Training? | Used Professionally? |
|-------|-------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------------|
```

**Transferable / Soft Skills**
```
| Skill | Evidence | Frequency of Use | Feedback Received |
|-------|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
```

**Hidden Skills** (things you do that you don't think of as skills):
- Organizing events
- Explaining complex things simply
- Mediating conflicts
- Learning new tools quickly
- Building relationships across cultures

### Skills That Transfer Across Roles

Help users see portability:
```
| Skill | Current Context | Could Apply To |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
```

## Career Vision

### Writing a Vision Statement

Not a goal — a direction. Guide users to write 2-3 sentences:

Template: "In [timeframe], I want to be [doing what] for [whom], in a way that [values expression], while [lifestyle condition]."

Example: "In 3 years, I want to be leading AI transformation projects for mid-size companies, in a way that combines my technical depth with my love of teaching, while having flexibility to be present for my family."

### The Three-Horizon Model

| Horizon | Timeframe | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| H1: Now | 0-6 months | What role/situation am I targeting next? |
| H2: Near | 1-2 years | What capabilities am I building toward? |
| H3: Far | 3-5 years | What does "success" look like? |
