## Onboarding System

### Creating a Role Record

When accepting an offer:
1. Create `roles/ROLE-NNN-Company-Title/`
2. Create `onboarding.md` with 30-60-90 day plan
3. Create `achievements/` folder
4. Create `reviews/` folder
5. Create `growth.md` for development tracking
6. Link back to the application folder for context

### 30-60-90 Day Plan

```markdown
# Onboarding Plan: [Company] — [Role]

**Start Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Manager:** [name]
**Buddy/Mentor:** [name]

---

## 30-Day Goals (Learn)
Focus: Understand the landscape. Build relationships. Don't try to change anything yet.

- [ ] Complete HR/IT onboarding
- [ ] Meet all direct team members 1:1
- [ ] Understand current architecture / systems / processes
- [ ] Identify key stakeholders and their priorities
- [ ] Read internal documentation (wikis, runbooks, design docs)
- [ ] Shadow a team member on their daily work
- [ ] Understand the team's current goals and OKRs

**30-Day Check-in Notes:**
[What did you learn? What surprised you? What questions remain?]

## 60-Day Goals (Contribute)
Focus: Start adding value. Take ownership of small, scoped work.

- [ ] Ship first meaningful contribution
- [ ] Own a small feature or project end-to-end
- [ ] Document one undocumented system or process
- [ ] Give feedback in at least one code/design review
- [ ] Build relationship with cross-functional partner

**60-Day Check-in Notes:**
[What value have you added? Where are you still ramping?]

## 90-Day Goals (Impact)
Focus: Demonstrate judgment. Identify improvement opportunities. Lead something.

- [ ] Lead a small project or initiative
- [ ] Identify and propose one improvement opportunity
- [ ] Present learnings or results to team
- [ ] Have career development conversation with manager
- [ ] Update Accomplishments-Tracker with new achievements

**90-Day Check-in Notes:**
[What impact have you made? What's your trajectory?]
```

## Achievement Tracking

### Why Track Achievements

- You will forget 80% of your accomplishments by review time
- Quantified results are impossible to reconstruct months later
- A rich achievement log makes resume updates trivial
- Performance reviews become copy-paste instead of panic-writing

### When to Log

Log immediately (within 24 hours) when you:
- Ship something significant
- Get positive feedback from a stakeholder (quote it verbatim)
- Solve a hard problem
- Receive metrics that show impact
- Complete a certification or course
- Lead a meeting, presentation, or initiative
- Mentor someone through a challenge
- Get recognized (award, shout-out, promotion)

### Achievement Record Template

```markdown
# Achievement: [Title]

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Role:** ROLE-NNN — [Title] @ [Company]
**Category:** [Technical | Leadership | Process | Business | Learning]

## Summary
[One sentence: what you did and why it mattered]

## Context (Situation)
[What was the problem? Why did it matter? Who cared?]

## What I Did (Action)
- [Specific actions taken]
- [Decisions made]
- [Tools or methods used]

## Results (Quantified)
- [Metric: before → after (% change)]
- [Business impact in dollars, users, time saved]
- [Scope: team size, number of systems, geographic reach]

## Evidence
- [ ] Link to PR/commit/doc
- [ ] Screenshot of metrics
- [ ] Feedback quote (who said it, when)
- [ ] Presentation or write-up

## Skills Demonstrated
- [Skill 1]
- [Skill 2]

## STAR-Ready?
- [ ] Can I tell this story in 2 minutes with a clear result?
- [ ] Marked as [INTERVIEW-READY] in Accomplishments-Tracker
```

### Quick Win (Lightweight)

For smaller achievements that still deserve tracking:

```markdown
# Win: [Title]
**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD | **Category:** [category]
**What:** [1-2 sentences]
**Impact:** [quantified if possible]
**Witness:** [who saw it]
```

## The Living Record

### Quarterly Review (15 Minutes)

Run every quarter, even when not searching:

1. **Achievement sweep** — Log anything missed from the last 3 months
2. **Accomplishments sync** — Copy new achievements to master Accomplishments-Tracker.md
3. **Growth check** — Update skill gaps and learning progress
4. **Narrative check** — Does your positioning story still reflect reality?
5. **Market pulse** — Note any new role types, salary bands, or in-demand skills

### Signals That Trigger Updates

- Shipped something significant → log achievement
- Positive feedback from stakeholder → log with quote
- Completed certification or course → log + update skills
- Received an offer (even if declined) → proof of market value
- Interview question you couldn't answer well → gap to close
- Reorganization or role change → update positioning
